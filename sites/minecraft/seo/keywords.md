## 6. AI Search Optimization (ChatGPT / Gemini / Perplexity)

AI-powered search engines (LLMs with retrieval) reward structured, factual, clearly-attributed content. Apply the following:

### 6.1 Answer-First Content Structure
Write content so the first sentence answers the query directly:
> "My Computer Academy — це онлайн та офлайн школа програмування для дітей від 6 до 16 років у Дніпрі, Україна."

### 6.2 Entity Clarity
- Mention the brand name, city, country, and specialization in the first paragraph of every page.
- Use consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all pages and Google Maps.

### 6.3 FAQ-Optimized Content
AI tools often surface FAQ content. Keep answers concise (2–4 sentences) and factual:
- "З якого віку можна записатись?" → "Від 6 років. Курс Scratch — для 6–10 річних, Python/Web/Roblox — для 10+."
- Match FAQ questions to real "People Also Ask" queries.

### 6.4 Structured Data (already implemented)
- `FAQPage` schema makes answers citable by AI retrieval systems.
- `Course` schema with `educationalLevel`, `audience`, and `timeRequired` properties.

### 6.5 Topical Authority Pages
Create supporting content that AI systems can cite:
- /blog/scratch-vs-python — comparison article
- /blog/yak-pochaty-prohramuvannya — beginner's guide
- /blog/chому-python-dlya-ditei — "why Python" explainer

### 6.6 Citability Signals
- Use precise, verifiable statistics: "98% задоволених сімей", "понад 500 випускників".
- Link to authoritative external sources (Scratch.mit.edu, Python.org) to signal topical depth.
- Avoid vague claims — AI retrieval systems deprioritize non-specific marketing language.
